## Title: 1984
A dystopian novel set in a totalitarian state where surveillance, propaganda, and fear control every part of life. Winston Smith begins to question the Party and searches for truth in a system that rewrites reality. The story explores freedom, manipulation, and resistance under oppression.

## Title: The Hobbit
Bilbo Baggins, a comfort-loving hobbit, is drawn into an unexpected quest with dwarves to reclaim a lost treasure guarded by a dragon. On the journey he faces danger, discovers inner courage, and forms unlikely friendships. Main themes include adventure, growth, loyalty, and bravery.

## Title: Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone
Harry discovers he is a wizard and enters Hogwarts, where he finds friendship, wonder, and hidden threats. Alongside Ron and Hermione, he uncovers a mystery linked to his past and a dark force returning to power. Themes include friendship, identity, courage, and magic.

## Title: The Lord of the Rings: The Fellowship of the Ring
A humble hobbit, Frodo, inherits a powerful ring tied to evil and begins a dangerous mission to destroy it. A diverse fellowship travels across Middle-earth while facing betrayal, temptation, and war. The book highlights friendship, sacrifice, hope, and the burden of responsibility.

## Title: To Kill a Mockingbird
Set in the American South, this novel follows Scout Finch as she witnesses racial injustice through her father Atticus's legal defense of a wrongly accused man. Childhood innocence clashes with prejudice and social inequality. Key themes are justice, empathy, morality, and courage.

## Title: Dune
On the desert planet Arrakis, Paul Atreides is caught in political conflict over the most valuable resource in the universe. As power struggles deepen, he faces prophecy, survival, and difficult choices. Themes include leadership, ecology, destiny, religion, and empire.

## Title: All Quiet on the Western Front
A young German soldier experiences the brutal reality of World War I, far from heroic ideals taught at home. The novel portrays fear, loss, and the psychological cost of war on ordinary people. Central themes are war trauma, disillusionment, friendship, and survival.

## Title: The Book Thief
In Nazi Germany, a young girl finds comfort in stealing books and sharing stories during bombing raids and persecution. Narrated by Death, the novel blends tenderness and tragedy in a time of violence. It explores the power of words, humanity, grief, and resilience.

## Title: Pride and Prejudice
Elizabeth Bennet navigates social expectations, family pressure, and first impressions while her relationship with Mr. Darcy evolves. Wit and emotional growth drive the story toward deeper understanding. Main themes are love, class, pride, prejudice, and personal change.

## Title: The Alchemist
A young shepherd named Santiago follows a recurring dream in search of treasure, meeting mentors and obstacles along the way. His journey becomes a spiritual search for purpose and self-knowledge. Themes include destiny, perseverance, intuition, and transformation.

## Title: The Name of the Wind
Kvothe recounts his life from gifted child to legendary figure, including years of poverty, study, and mystery at a university of magic. The narrative combines music, myth, and ambition with danger. Themes include talent, obsession, identity, storytelling, and magic.

## Title: The Nightingale
During World War II in occupied France, two sisters take different paths of resistance and survival under Nazi rule. Their choices reveal courage, sacrifice, and the hidden roles of women in wartime. Themes include war, family, resilience, and moral bravery.
